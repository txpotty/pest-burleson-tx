A Detailed Look at Our Comprehensive Wildlife Control Services

Wildlife control is a multi-faceted service that requires a thorough understanding of animal behavior, building structures, and effective removal techniques. Our comprehensive approach at Burleson Pest Pros addresses every aspect of a wildlife problem, from identification and removal to repair and prevention. We don't believe in quick fixes; we focus on long-lasting solutions that protect your property from future intrusions.

Initial Inspection and Assessment

The first step in effective wildlife control is a detailed inspection. Our trained technicians will thoroughly examine your property, both inside and out, to identify the type of animal present, locate entry points, and assess the extent of the damage. This crucial step allows us to develop a customized plan tailored to your specific situation.

Identifying the Intruder

Different animals require different removal strategies. Whether you're dealing with squirrels in the attic, raccoons in the garbage, or bats in the eaves, our team has the expertise to accurately identify the species causing the problem. Knowing the animal's habits and behaviors is essential for effective and humane removal.

Humane Wildlife Removal

Once the animal and entry points are identified, we implement humane removal techniques. This often involves setting traps designed to capture the animal safely without causing harm. We follow strict protocols for checking traps regularly and ensuring the well-being of the captured animal until it can be safely relocated.

Safe Trapping Methods

We utilize a variety of traps, including live traps, designed to capture the animal without injury. The type of trap used is determined by the species, the location of the problem, and the specific circumstances of the infestation. Our technicians are skilled in setting and monitoring these traps effectively.

Relocation Protocols

Once an animal is captured, we follow established protocols for relocation. This typically involves transporting the animal a safe distance away to a suitable habitat where it can thrive without returning to your property. Our priority is the humane treatment of wildlife throughout the entire process.

Exclusion and Prevention Strategies

Removing the animal is only part of the solution. To prevent future problems, it's essential to seal off all entry points and implement preventative measures. This is where our exclusion services come in. We identify and repair any openings that wildlife could use to access your home or business.

Sealing Entry Points

Wildlife can find their way into surprisingly small openings. We meticulously inspect your roofline, eaves, vents, chimneys, foundation, and other potential access points. We then use durable materials to seal these openings, creating a barrier that wildlife cannot penetrate. This might involve installing mesh over vents, repairing damaged flashing, or reinforcing weak areas.

Habitat Modification

Sometimes, the surrounding environment can attract wildlife to your property. We can provide recommendations for habitat modification, such as trimming tree branches that provide easy access to your roof or securing garbage cans, to make your property less appealing to unwanted visitors.

Damage Repair and Sanitation

Wildlife infestations can cause significant damage to your property, including insulation damage, electrical wiring damage, and structural compromise. They also leave behind droppings and urine, which can pose health risks. Our services include damage repair and thorough sanitation to restore your property to a safe and healthy condition.

Repairing Structural Damage

Our team can repair damage caused by gnawing, nesting, or burrowing. This might involve repairing holes in walls or roofs, replacing damaged insulation, or addressing other structural issues compromised by the animals. Our goal is to restore the integrity of your property.

Sanitizing Contaminated Areas

Wildlife droppings and urine contain harmful pathogens and can create unpleasant odors. We perform thorough sanitation of affected areas, removing waste and applying disinfectants to eliminate health risks and odors. This is a vital step in restoring your property to a clean and safe state.

Common Problems, Signs You Need Wildlife Control in Burleson, TX, and How We Help

Wildlife encounters are not uncommon in the Burleson area, given the blend of urban and natural environments. Recognizing the signs of a wildlife problem early can prevent extensive damage and health risks. If you notice any of the following, it's likely time to consider professional wildlife control in Burleson, TX services from Burleson Pest Pros. We have seen it all and are prepared to tackle any wildlife challenge you face, whether it's a lone squirrel or a family of raccoons.

Strange Noises in Your Walls or Attic

Hearing scratching, scurrying, or gnawing sounds from inside your walls, ceiling, or attic, especially at night or in the early morning, is a strong indicator of wildlife presence. Animals like rodents, squirrels, or even raccoons may be nesting or moving around within these spaces. These noises are not only unsettling but can also point to potential damage being done to your home's structure or electrical wiring. Our team can identify the source of these noises and safely remove the animals causing them, followed by inspecting and repairing any resulting damage. We understand that these sounds can be distressing, interrupting your sleep and peace of mind. Addressing them promptly is key to preventing further issues and restoring a quiet, comfortable home environment.

Visible Damage to Your Property

Look for physical signs of wildlife activity such as chewed wires, gnawed wood, damaged vents, torn insulation, or disturbed roof shingles. Wildlife, particularly rodents and squirrels, often gnaw on materials to build nests or access food and water, leading to significant structural or electrical damage. Raccoons can be quite strong and may tear at materials to gain entry. These visible signs are clear indicators that wildlife has breached your home's exterior or is causing destruction within. Prompt action is necessary to prevent the damage from worsening, which could lead to expensive repairs down the line. Our experts are skilled at identifying the type of damage and attributing it to a specific animal, allowing us to implement the most effective removal and repair strategy. Addressing this damage is not just about aesthetics; it's about maintaining the safety and integrity of your home.

Unpleasant Odors

Persistent foul odors coming from your attic, walls, or other areas of your home can be a result of animal droppings, urine, or even a deceased animal. These smells are not only unpleasant but can also indicate the presence of harmful pathogens and bacteria. Wildlife waste can seep into materials, creating lingering odors that are difficult to remove without professional cleaning and sanitization. The longer the issue goes unaddressed, the more pervasive and ingrained the odors will become, making your home an uncomfortable environment. Our wildlife control service includes thorough sanitation and deodorization of affected areas, eliminating both the source of the odor and the associated health risks. We utilize specialized cleaning agents to ensure that all traces of waste are removed, leaving your home fresh and clean. Dealing with these odors requires more than just masking them; it requires a complete cleanup to restore a healthy living space.

Discovering Droppings or Nests

Finding animal droppings in or around your home, or discovering nests made of leaves, twigs, or insulation in your attic, walls, or crawl spaces, are definite signs of wildlife infestation. The size and shape of the droppings can help identify the type of animal present, guiding our removal strategy. Nests indicate that animals are not just passing through but have taken up residence on your property, potentially preparing to breed. These nests can also clog vents and create fire hazards, in addition to being unsanitary. Discovering these signs confirms an active infestation and highlights the need for professional intervention to safely remove the animals and their nesting materials. Ignoring these signs will likely lead to a growing problem and increased damage. Our team is experienced in safely removing nests and cleaning up droppings, minimizing the risk of exposure to harmful pathogens. Proper disposal of these materials is critical for preventing the spread of disease. It's important to address nesting sites not only for removal but also to prevent re-infestation.

Seeing Wildlife on Your Property During the Day

While some wildlife is naturally nocturnal, seeing animals like raccoons or possums regularly on your property during daylight hours can indicate that they have become comfortable and are actively seeking food or shelter. This increased visibility suggests a more established presence and potentially boldness from the animal, which can increase the risk of conflict with pets or people. Animals that are not exhibiting their natural behaviors may be indicating a disruption in their environment or a reliance on your property as a food source or shelter. Their daytime presence could also signal that they are sick or injured, which poses additional risks. Active sightings during the day are a clear signal that a wildlife problem is developing or has become a regular issue that requires professional attention to manage safely. Seeing these animals frequently can be alarming and disrupt your ability to enjoy your outdoor space. Addressing their presence is crucial for both safety and comfort. It's important to mitigate factors attracting them during the day.

Pets Behaving Aggressively or Acting Skittish

If your pets are suddenly agitated, constantly barking or growling at certain areas of your home (like walls or the attic), or seem unusually skittish, it could be a sign that they sense or hear wildlife inside. Pets have keen senses and often react to the presence of animals that you might not even be aware of yet. Their change in behavior is a subtle but important indicator that something is disturbing them within your home's structure. This can be a valuable early warning sign of an infestation before more obvious signs like noises or damage become apparent. Paying attention to your pets' reactions can help you identify a potential wildlife issue early, allowing for prompt investigation and resolution. Their instincts can be a helpful tool in detecting hidden problems. Observing their behavior around different parts of the house can narrow down potential problem areas.

Damaged Gardens or Landscaping

Wildlife can cause significant damage to gardens, lawns, and landscaping while foraging for food or materials for nests. Uprooted plants, dug-up soil, and damaged trees or shrubs are common signs of animal activity. Animals like squirrels, rabbits, and even deer can decimate gardens and landscaping, destroying your hard work and investment. This damage isn't just about aesthetics; it can also indicate that your property is an attractive food source for wildlife, which could lead to animals attempting to enter your home for shelter. Protecting your landscaping and garden requires addressing the underlying reason why wildlife is attracted to that area. Our wildlife control strategies can help deter animals from your outdoor spaces, protecting your plants and maintaining the beauty of your property. We can identify which animals are causing the damage and implement targeted solutions. Preventing this damage is important for preserving your outdoor living space.
